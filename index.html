Hi, I'm Eileen. I'm a biologist committed to 
sustainability & biodiversity conservation.

    I grew up in Argentina, lived in Ireland and I´m currently based in Germany.

    My work is driven by a strong belief that the solution to today’s most pressing environmental challenges involves incorporating sustainability into mainstream cultures, through top-down and bottom-up change, and redefining our relationship with nature.

    I currently work as a coordinator for biodiversity and sustainability projects and outreach programmes – which day in and day out entails carrying out desktop research, creating media for a diverse range of audiences, participating as a speaker in nature tours and contributing to ecology surveys with my computer and field skills.
    Because of my broad academic training and international background, I bring to the table a multidimensional and systemic perspective – grasp the details, see the bigger picture and draw relevant connections to activate pragmatic and value-based solutions.

    When I’m not working, I’m most likely volunteering in my local bats’ group, nursing baby bats at home, or enabling sustainability in my daily life – whether it is learning to grow and forage food, formulate cosmetics or reduce my consumption footprint.

    Reach out if any of this sounds interesting to you or if you have a good book to recommend.
